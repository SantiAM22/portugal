Introducion:

Portuguese gastronomy is known for its rich flavors and fresh ingredients. Anchored by seafood like salted cod (bacalhau), hearty soups such as caldo verde, and beloved pastries like pastel de nata, Portugal’s cuisine blends simple preparations with deep regional traditions. Olive oil, local produce, and age-old recipes make Portuguese food both comforting and distinct

Cataplana:

Caldo verde:

Ingredients:

layering ingredients, typically onions, garlic, peppers, tomatoes, white wine, and fresh seafood (clams, prawns, monkfish)—in a special hinged, clam-shaped copper pot

 

Preparation:

1:Clean shellfish thoroughly to remove sand. Slice onions, garlic, and bell peppers, and chop tomatoes.
In the base of the open cataplana pan, coat with olive oil and layer the aromatic vegetables, followed by potatoes, then fish, and top with clams and prawns.
Season with salt, pepper, paprika or piri-piri sauce, bay leaves, and fresh coriander. Drizzle generously with white wine and more olive oil.
Close the cataplana. Cook on low-medium heat for 15-20 minutes, allowing the steamer to cook the seafood until the shellfish open and the fish is tender.
Shake the closed pot once or twice during cooking. Serve directly from the closed, steaming pot at the table

Pastel de nata:

Migas a la alentejana:

Ingredients:

Pastry.
Custard.
Thickener.

Preparation:

1:Prepare the Dough:Roll puff pastry into a thin rectangle and roll it tightly into a long, thin log.
Cut the log into 1-inch (3cm) pieces.
2:Shape the Tins:Dip your thumbs in water and press the dough from the center outwards, keeping the bottom thin and creating a slightly thicker, raised rim.
3:Make the Sugar Syrup:Boil sugar, water, cinnamon stick, and lemon peel for 3–5 minutes until it reaches roughly 106°C/220°F (a light syrup).
4:Make the Custard:Heat the milk mixture until it thickens.
Slowly pour the warm sugar syrup into the thickened milk, stirring constantly.
Let it cool for 10–15 minutes, then whisk in egg yolks.
5:Bake:Preheat the oven to its highest setting (500°F–550°F / 250°C–290°C).
Fill the pastry cases 3/4 full with custard.
Bake for 8–12 minutes, or until the custard is set and the tops are dark brown and blistered.

Ingredients:

Pan: 500g-1kg de pan alentejano, rústico o de pueblo, de 2 o 3 días anteriores.
Carne: Entrecot de cerdo, panceta o lomo.
Ajo: 4 a 6 dientes de ajo.
Grasa: Manteca de cerdo o aceite de oliva virgen extra.

Preparation:

1:Preparar el pan
-Corta el pan duro en trozos medianos.
-Remójalo unos minutos en agua caliente.
-Escúrrelo bien y resérvalo.
2:Marinar la carne
-Sazona la carne de cerdo con sal, ajo, pimentón, laurel y vino blanco
-Cocinar la carne
-Calienta manteca o aceite en una sartén.
-Fríe la carne hasta que esté bien dorada.
-Retírala y reserva, dejando la grasa en la sartén.
3:Sofreír los aromáticos
-En la misma grasa, sofríe ajo picado.
-Cocinar las migas
-Añade el pan escurrido.
-Remueve y machaca con cuchara de madera.
4:Cocina a fuego bajo hasta que las migas estén sueltas y doradas.
